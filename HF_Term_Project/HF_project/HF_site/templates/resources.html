Accessing Shelter or Housing services

If you are at risk of or currently experiencing homelessness, the first step is to get in touch with the shelter system in your community. There are numerous resources available, such as hotlines or community organizations for homeless services. Many communities have a "homeless hotline," "2-1-1, " or other organizations/agencies that serve as the "front door" to receiving any kind of help.

Getting in Touch with the Shelter System in Your Community

A 2-1-1 hotline is an option in many areas and provides insights from trained professionals who can assist with services such as finding shelter, housing, or other resources.
Find your community's 2-1-1 hotline | Call 2-1-1
The National Coalition for the Homeless offers basic information on how people experiencing homelessness can get help and access to resources.
If your community does not have the above resources easily available, a good place to start looking for various providers is the County Department of Human or Social services, a nearby church, social service non-profit, library, or a food pantry.

Exploring Options Other Than Shelter

Safe alternatives to shelters are often a much better way of addressing or resolving housing crises. Many provider staff from hotlines may try to help you maintain your current housing and resolve conflicts that are keeping you from staying there. If you are not yet homeless, it may be possible to avoid homelessness by finding out about prevention or emergency assistance programs in your area, which can help with paying rent, utilities, or other bills.
